Portfolio

Hey, so here's some of my most recent work! It starts off with my client work, then delves into my personal projects, and finally wraps up with some of my open source contributions.

Client Work

Vayable

Vayable is a crowdsourced local touring site designed for travelers to find their ideal experience away from home. Hired on contract as frontend/rails developer. Designed and created their mobile site as well as adding a couple booking features to their main site.

Winston Wacter

Along with two designers, I created this new digital home for the Winston Wachter galleries. Based out of Seattle and NY, we were tasked with presenting both galleries in under two weeks.

  • WordPress

ByUs

Worked in a team of four to bring this new startup's beta to life. ByUs offers individuals a more personalized and trusted version of yelp.

My primary role was as a frontend/rails developer.

LittleCast

Managed and worked with an overseas team to build this video publishing site. The core tech was built on top of rails and highly integrated with facebook.

Please get in touch, if you'd like to get a full history of my client work.

Personal Projects

ShopOrNot

My current project and first publicly released iOS app. I spent a while developing this shopping application from the ground up. After continued usage, ShopOrNot develops a style profile for the user and provides them with recommendations.

  • Rails/Postgres backend
  • Customized PredictionIO backend
  • iOS 8

TapToUnlock

Mobile safari optimized iPhone unlocking tool. Customer would fly through IMEI unlocking by installing a secure provisioning certificate. Unfortuantely, I had to discontinue this project due to nature of unlocking industry (legality, shady providers, etc).

  • Mobile Safari Optimized
  • Rails/Postgres

Memstash

Memstash is a tool that uses intermittent reptition to allow you memorize anything from the web. Select a piece of text or an image and use the javascript bookmarklet to get scheduled reminders. Two others (BC & Sina) and I built this at Techcrunch Disrupt.

  • Rails
  • Techrunch Evernote Prize Winner

I'd love to revisit this at some point on iOS + WatchKit.

Hooked.By

A friend and I developed this social content network over the course of a month or two. Despite getting a decent amount of traffic from Stumbleupon (would highly recommend their advertising platform for anyone with similiar content), we realized the ad yeild was too low and writing click baity titles all day wasn't our sort of thang.

While the site does continues to churn, it does so without supervision - heh.

Open Source Contributions

FixTheDMCA

Our laws regarding the ownership and use of our digital devices are poorly constructed; we need reform. I wrote a large portion of the front-end.

The Day We Fight Back

On February 11, 2014 thousands of websites added a banner asked their visitors to call or email their representatives. As part of Taskforce, I helped write the front-end of the thedaywefightback.js banner.

StopWatchingUs

Organized by Taskforce.is and Mozilla, StopWatchingUs was a campaign that enabled individuals to reach out to their constiuents and express their thoughts on the unlawful surveillance by the NSA.

Taskforce.is

Taskforce.is a volunteer group of internet activists who create campaigns in response to issues regarding online privacy and technology privacy.

Quantified Mind

One of my first open source contributions, I helped Yoni flesh out the frontend of this quantified-self tool. I now just have waay too much data about myself - it'll come in handy one day I keep telling myself.
